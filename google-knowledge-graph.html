How Does Google Knowledge Graph Works

Data is stored as entities (nodes) and edges in the graph database model used by Google's Knowledge Graph. Entities include distinct things or concepts like people, places, and things, as well as intangibles like ideas, movies, TV shows, and other things.

The edges connecting each node to various other entities create a non-linear hierarchy of data structures that depicts the link between nodes. By storing the information in various formats, Google is better able to select the correct result to display. Google continuously expands the web by exploring both implicitly and overtly.

When a user types a question into the search box, Google algorithms quickly explore the Knowledge Graph to discover the most pertinent result. They do this by understanding the semantics of the user's words. The majority of the time, inquiries are answered straight on the search result pages without the need to click on any of the links that are prominently shown there.

The Google Knowledge Graph is largely to blame for the SERP's regular inclusion of rich results. Featured Snippet, Knowledge Graph Panel, Knowledge card, Direct responses, and Image carousel are just a few examples of rich results. However, the Knowledge Panel Graph has largely captivated everyone's attention because it is aesthetically pleasing and takes up a lot of space on the SERP.
